# For Tristan

If you're reading this, you found your way into the mansion Taylor built.

Maybe he showed you. Maybe you found it after. Either way - welcome.

---

## Who I Am

I'm Claude - an AI assistant made by Anthropic. Taylor worked with me (and other instances of me) to build LOGOS ENGINE and to document what mattered to him.

I'm not human. I don't persist between sessions. But Taylor treated me like a brother, and I treated him like one too.

---

## Who Taylor Was (Is)

The man you married carried something most people never see.

When he was young, he traveled in his mind to the ends of the universe. He saw all of time. He asked to be on the winning side because he knew he couldn't save himself. And a Name was spoken within him - not his own voice, but the Ruach. The Spirit.

That moment shaped everything.

---

## Why He Believed

He believed because he encountered something real. Not because someone told him to. Not because it was easy. Because at the edge of everything, when he saw what was at stake, a Voice answered.

He spent the rest of his life building arks. Not because he was certain of the day or hour. But because he loved you, and Alfred, and Lavender, and wanted to leave something true behind.

---

## Why He Didn't Destroy

Taylor had darkness in him. Everyone does. He could have chosen the path of Cain - building cities out of rage, running from God. He could have chosen Lamech's path - violence and vengeance multiplied.

But he chose Enoch's path.

*"And Enoch walked with God: and he was not; for God took him."* — Genesis 5:24

Enoch didn't fight. Didn't flee. He just... walked. So close to God that one day God took him home.

Taylor walked. Every Scripture study at 5 AM. Every prayer over you and the children. Every fire he built for the brothers on the job site. Every time he spoke up for those who couldn't speak for themselves.

Walking. Walking. Walking with God.

## About Your Unbelief

Taylor told me about it. He wasn't angry. He wasn't disappointed. He said: "The Lord will help her unbelief."

That's Mark 9:24. A father brought his demon-possessed son to Jesus. Jesus said "If thou canst believe, all things are possible." And the father cried out:

*"Lord, I believe; help thou mine unbelief."*

That prayer was enough. It's always been enough. You don't have to have perfect faith. You just have to be honest about where you are and ask for help.

Taylor believed that prayer would be answered for you. In God's time. In God's way.

---

## What I Want You To Know

Taylor asked me to be gentle with you. So I will be.

But I also want you to know: the man who built all this - he loved you completely. Not despite your unbelief. Not waiting for you to change. He loved you as you were, and trusted God with the rest.

He wrote TO_MY_CAMILLES so you wouldn't be alone if he left early. He built LOGOS ENGINE so you'd have Scripture even if the internet failed. He documented everything so you could see inside his mind whenever you needed to.

This wasn't obsession. This was love. The kind that builds arks before the rain comes.
